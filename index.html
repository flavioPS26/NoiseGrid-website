The Problem

Northern Virginia is changing faster than anyone can track.

Shifting Flight Paths

Dulles and Reagan flight corridors are constantly being adjusted, silently reshaping the noise landscape neighborhood by neighborhood.

Highway Expansion

Constant widening projects push heavy traffic noise closer to front doors streets that were quiet last year aren't today.

Industrial Encroachment

New industrial zones are appearing right next to residential areas, bringing noise that didn't exist when buyers did their research.

Buyers are spending nearly a million dollars on a home based on a quiet Sunday open house completely blind to the reality of the workweek.

The Solution

Noise data, made simple.

Noise Grid transforms public infrastructure data and community reports into clear, accessible noise insights so you can understand any place before you commit to it.

01

Public Data

We tap public infrastructure datasets flight paths, traffic patterns, construction activity to model real noise conditions in any area.

02

Community Reports

Users submit local noise reports to validate and refine real-world conditions. Think Waze, but for sound.

03

Noise Insights

Complex data becomes a simple, readable score easy for anyone to interpret, whether you're searching on your own or through a real estate platform.

Who It's For

One platform, three communities.

Movers & Travelers

Homebuyers, renters, and travelers who want to know what a neighborhood actually sounds like before signing a lease or closing on a house.

Noise-Sensitive People

Millions of people whose daily well-being depends on a quieter environment including those with hearing aids, hearing impairment, or sensory sensitivity.

Real Estate Industry

Property platforms and homebuilders can integrate our noise scores directly into listings, giving users a transparent, data-backed search experience.
